Pirates of the Caribbean: At World’s End (2007)

Recommendable

Metrics

8.4 /10

People who like action movies. Kids, teens, adults.

Rewatch Time:

Once a month is possible, it's just great. 2h45.

Winner:

Captain Jack Sparrow (Johnny Depp)

Review

I don't know why exactly people don't like this one so much - it might be too outlandish for some, I guess - but I definitely thought it was one of the most awesome movies I'd ever seen when I first watched it. Depp's Captain Jack is still awesome, supported by a great cast of Lord Beckett (Tom Hollander), Davy Jones (Bill Nighy), Captain Barbossa (Geoffrey Rush), Will Turner (Orlando Bloom), and Elizabeth Swann (Keira Knightley). Then the supporting characters like Gibbs (Kevin McNally), Tia Dalma (Naomie Harris), Pintel and Ragetti, Mullroy and Murtogg, Bootstrap Bill (Stellan Skarsgard), and James Norrington (Jack Davenport) are also iconic. The introduction of Singapore and Sao Feng's (Chow Yunfat) crew was really cool leading up to the Brethren Court, which I just wholly enjoyed. Action was great, all the plotting was awesome, the characters were all handled really well, especially given the cliffhanger from the last movie, and it's always a thrill to watch.

Spoiler Territory

Elizabeth's speech and the whole final battle was to die for. I will say I would have liked a little less Calypso stuff, but it was good character building for Davy Jones. Also Keith Richards as Jack's dad was hilarious, since he was the inspiration for Depp's mannerisms. But yeah, Barbossa was almost as wonderful as Jack, their dialogue is actually really meaningful, and the resolution to the arc was all pretty good - it made us like Will more than we would have otherwise. Beckett was a really annoying person, but we love to hate him, even when he's bullying Davy Jones. Elizabeth is also just a badass in this one. Oh, also, the whole song stuff is really cool mood-building, but the Locker scene with the hallucinations and crabs was weird.
